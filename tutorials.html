Blend of agile/freelance for clarity.

Freelancing can be intimidating - you don't know who the person is. What if you could specify what you want to be built? What if the developer doesn't build the right tool? Charging based off projects is the traditional approach, but my payment schedules are based off features developed.

This way, if you want to test the skillset of the developer or if you change your mind on the project execution - the schedule would ensure satisfaction.

On the other hand, if you only want some parts built and want to give the rest to another developer, you can do that. This establishes mutual trust and a rest of mind.

  • Build what you want.
  • Agile-freelance blend.
  • Ensures development to the detail.
  • Eliminates ambiguity.
